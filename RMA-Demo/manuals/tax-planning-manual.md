# Tax Planning Manual for Advisors

## Chapter 1: Income Tax Allowances

### Personal Allowance (2024-2025)

The Personal Allowance is the amount of income you can earn before paying income tax.

Current rates:
- Basic Personal Allowance: £12,570
- Increased rate (age 65-74): £12,570
- Additional allowance (age 75+): £12,570
- Scotland: £12,570

Withdrawal of allowance:
- Personal allowance is withdrawn at £1 for every £2 earned above £100,000
- If income exceeds £125,140, no personal allowance remains

### Marriage Allowance

Marriage Allowance allows one spouse to transfer their unused allowance to the other spouse.

Key features:
- One spouse must be non-taxpayer (income below £12,570)
- Other spouse must be basic rate taxpayer (income £12,571 to £50,270)
- Benefit: Up to £252 per year (£1,260 transferred at 20%)
- Can be claimed up to 4 years back
- Automatic application through self-assessment

Eligibility check:
1. Check both spouses' income levels
2. Confirm marriage or civil partnership
3. Ensure neither receives benefits that would disqualify
4. Calculate potential saving

### Blind Person's Allowance

An additional allowance available to blind people.

Amount: £2,520 (2024-2025)
- Added to personal allowance (total: £15,090)
- Not withdrawn if blind (even with high income)
- Can be transferred to spouse if unused

Requirements:
- Registered as blind with local authority
- British resident
- Normal residence in UK for tax purposes

## Chapter 2: Capital Gains Tax

### Annual Exemption

The annual exemption allows you to make gains without paying capital gains tax.

2024-2025 rates:
- Individual: £3,000
- Trustees: £1,500
- Most estates: £3,000

### Capital Gains Tax Rates

For disposals on or after 2024-2025:

Gains on most assets:
- Basic rate taxpayer: 20%
- Higher rate taxpayer: 20%
- Additional rate taxpayer: 20%

Gains on residential property:
- Basic rate taxpayer: 8%
- Higher rate taxpayer: 14%
- Additional rate taxpayer: 20%

Gains on carried interest:
- Basic rate taxpayer: 20%
- Higher rate taxpayer: 20%
- Additional rate taxpayer: 20%

### Lifetime Allowance Relief

Lifetime allowance relief applies to:
- Gains made on or after 6 April 2023
- Where lifetime allowance was previously breached
- Provides relief of 25% of the excess over the standard rate

## Chapter 3: Dividend Tax

### Dividend Allowance

An annual allowance exists for dividend income.

Dividend Allowance (2024-2025):
- Basic rate: £1,000
- Higher rate: £500
- Additional rate: £500

### Dividend Tax Rates

Dividend income above the allowance is taxed at:

Basic rate taxpayer:
- First: £50,270 income
- Dividend tax rate: 8.75%

Higher rate taxpayer:
- Income from £50,271 to £125,140
- Dividend tax rate: 33.75%

Additional rate taxpayer:
- Income above £125,140
- Dividend tax rate: 39.35%

### Dividend Income Planning

Strategies to manage dividend tax:
1. Split income with spouse (marriage allowance)
2. Make gift Aid charitable donations
3. Use ISAs for investments (tax-free dividends)
4. Consider timing of dividend distributions
5. Use Enterprise Investment Scheme (EIS)

## Chapter 4: Savings Interest

### Personal Savings Allowance

An allowance on interest from savings accounts.

Rates (2024-2025):
- Basic rate taxpayer: £1,000
- Higher rate taxpayer: £500
- Additional rate taxpayer: £0

### Starting Rate for Savings

A reduced rate applies to starting rate taxpayer.

- Income up to £12,570 (part of personal allowance)
- First £5,000 can be savings income at 0%
- Applies if other income is below threshold

Application:
- Must claim through tax return
- Not automatically applied
- Applies to interest on savings only (not dividends)

### Peer-to-Peer Lending Interest

Interest from P2P lending is taxable as savings income.

Relief available:
- Personal Savings Allowance applies
- Starting Rate for Savings applies if eligible
- Can claim loss relief if loan defaults

## Chapter 5: Pension Relief

### Annual Allowance

The amount you can save in pensions with tax relief.

2024-2025 limit: £60,000
- Reduced to £40,000 if flexibly accessed funds
- Tapered allowance for high earners
- Individual charge on excess

High earner taper:
- Income above £260,100
- Reduced by £1 allowance per £2 of excess income
- Minimum allowance: £10,000

### Lifetime Allowance Relief

Note: Lifetime allowance was removed 14 April 2023
- Relief available for amounts over standard rate
- Applies to lump sums and pension transfers
- Relief of 25% for amounts above standard rate

### Pension Contribution Relief

Relief available for pension contributions:

Employed:
- Personal contributions get basic relief (20%)
- Employer contributions are not taxable
- Self-employed get full relief at their rate

Self-employed:
- Relief at marginal rate (20%, 40%, 45%)
- Net cost varies by tax rate
- Can claim in next tax year

## Chapter 6: Married Couples' Allowance

An additional allowance for married couples where one partner born before 6 April 1935.

2024-2025 allowance: £1,220
- One spouse must be born before 6 April 1935
- Married/in civil partnership
- Living together
- Both UK residents

Benefit calculation:
- Basic relief: 10% of allowance = £122
- Can be claimed by either spouse
- Unused portion transfers to spouse

## Chapter 7: Gift Aid

Charitable donations with tax relief.

Relief mechanism:
- Charity recovers 25% from HMRC (20% rate)
- Donor can reclaim higher/additional rate relief
- Gift Aid form must be completed

Donor reclaim:
- Higher rate taxpayer: Additional 20% relief
- Additional rate: Additional 25% relief

Example:
- £80 donation (after basic rate relief)
- Charity receives: £100
- Donor pays: £80
- If additional rate taxpayer, reclaim: £20

## Chapter 8: ISA Rules

### Cash ISAs

Tax-free savings accounts for cash deposits.

- Interest earned is completely tax-free
- No Personal Savings Allowance needed
- Multiple accounts allowed in one provider
- Contribution limit: £20,000 per tax year

### Stocks and Shares ISAs

Tax-free investment accounts.

- Capital gains are completely tax-free
- Dividends earned are completely tax-free
- Can hold individual shares, funds, ETFs
- Contribution limit: £20,000 per tax year

### Lifetime ISA

ISA for under 40s with government bonus.

- Save up to £4,000 per year
- Government adds 25% bonus (£1,000)
- Can only withdraw after age 60 or death
- Also available for first-time homebuyers

### Junior ISA

ISA account for children under 18.

- Opened by parent/guardian
- £9,000 annual contribution limit
- Interest and gains are tax-free
- Can only withdraw funds after age 18
